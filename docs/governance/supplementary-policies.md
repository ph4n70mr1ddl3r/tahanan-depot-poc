### POL-FIN-008: Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All employees, officers, directors, contractors, and agents across all entities  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** Advanced Controls, Expenses, Procurement

#### Policy Statement

Tahanan Depot maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward bribery and corruption in all forms, in compliance with Philippine law (Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act / RA 3019, Anti-Plunder Act / RA 7080), the United Nations Convention Against Corruption, and applicable international standards.

#### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Bribery | Offering, promising, giving, requesting, or accepting any financial or non-financial advantage to influence a business decision |
| Facilitation Payment | Small payment made to expedite a routine government action (prohibited under this policy) |
| Public Official | Any person holding a legislative, executive, administrative, or judicial office; includes government employees, LGU officials, BIR/BOC officers, police, and military |
| Kickback | Unauthorized payment or benefit received in return for awarding business or favoring a supplier |

#### Prohibited Conduct

1. Offering, promising, or giving bribes to any person (public or private) to obtain or retain business.
2. Requesting, agreeing to receive, or accepting bribes from any person.
3. Making facilitation payments to government officials to expedite routine actions.
4. Political contributions made to obtain or retain business advantage.
5. Charitable donations or sponsorships made to influence business decisions.
6. Providing gifts, hospitality, or entertainment beyond the thresholds defined in POL-HR-008 (Code of Conduct).
7. Using intermediaries, agents, or consultants to channel bribes on behalf of the company.
8. Retaliation against any employee who reports a bribery concern in good faith.

#### Gifts and Hospitality Limits

| Recipient Type | Gift Limit (per event) | Hospitality Limit (per event) | Annual Aggregate |
|---------------|----------------------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| Government Official | PHP 1,000 (token items only) | PHP 2,000 (working meals only) | PHP 5,000 |
| Private Sector Business Partner | PHP 2,000 (per POL-HR-008) | PHP 5,000 | PHP 10,000 |
| Employee | PHP 2,000 (per POL-HR-008) | N/A | N/A |

All gifts and hospitality above PHP 500 to government officials must be pre-approved by the CLO.

#### Due Diligence on Third Parties

1. All agents, consultants, distributors, and intermediaries representing Tahanan Depot undergo anti-bribery due diligence before engagement.
2. Due diligence includes: identity verification, reputation check, conflict of interest screening, and anti-bribery certifications.
3. High-risk third parties (those interacting with government officials on behalf of Tahanan Depot) require enhanced due diligence and CLO approval.
4. Contracts with third parties include anti-bribery representations, warranties, and audit rights.

#### Reporting and Investigation

1. Concerns reported via Integrity Helpline (anonymous), direct report to CLO, or HR.
2. All reports investigated within 30 days by Legal and Internal Audit.
3. Substantiated violations result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and referral to law enforcement.
4. Annual anti-bribery risk assessment conducted by Internal Audit with results reported to Board Audit Committee.

### POL-FIN-009: Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All entities and all employees handling financial transactions  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** Cash Management, Receivables, Payables, Expenses

#### Policy Statement

Tahanan Depot is committed to complying with the Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2001 (RA 9160, as amended), its Implementing Rules and Regulations (AMLA IRR), and related BSP regulations. As a business handling significant cash volumes (PHP 45B annual revenue), Tahanan Depot implements Know Your Customer (KYC), Customer Due Diligence (CDD), and suspicious transaction reporting procedures.

#### Covered Transactions

Tahanan Depot is classified as a covered person under AMLA for certain activities:

| Activity | AMLA Classification | Threshold |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Cash transactions exceeding PHP 500,000 (single or series) | Covered Transaction Report (CTR) | > PHP 500,000 within 1 business day |
| Suspicious transactions (any amount) | Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) | Any amount |
| B2B credit accounts | CDD required | All credit accounts |
| Gift card transactions | Monitoring required | All gift card sales/redemptions > PHP 500,000 cumulative |
| Cash deposits from stores | Monitoring required | All store deposits |

#### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

| Customer Type | CDD Requirements | Verification |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Walk-in cash customers | None (below PHP 500,000 threshold) | N/A |
| Loyalty members | Basic CDD: Name, address, date of birth, government ID | POS enrollment verification |
| B2B credit accounts | Enhanced CDD: SEC/DTI registration, BIR TIN, business address, authorized signatories, beneficial owner | Document verification by AR team |
| B2B cash customers (single transaction > PHP 500K) | One-time CDD: Name, address, government ID, purpose of transaction | Manager verification |
| Gift card bulk purchases (> PHP 100K) | Basic CDD: Name, address, government ID | Store Manager verification |

#### Suspicious Transaction Indicators

| Red Flag Category | Examples |
|-----------------|---------|
| Transaction patterns | Structured cash purchases just below PHP 500K threshold; frequent round-amount transactions; purchases inconsistent with customer profile |
| Customer behavior | Customer reluctant to provide identification; customer provides false or misleading information; customer accompanied by unknown third party |
| Payment behavior | Insistence on cash payment for large transactions; payment from multiple unrelated accounts; request to split invoices to avoid thresholds |
| Store-specific | Frequent returns and repurchases; gift card purchases with immediate redemption at different locations |

#### Reporting Obligations

| Report Type | Filing Deadline | Filing Method | Responsible |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| Covered Transaction Report (CTR) | Within 5 working days from transaction | AMLC online system | VP Finance / VP Treasury |
| Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) | Within 5 working days from detection | AMLC online system | VP Finance / CLO |
| No report filed (monthly) | 15th of following month | AMLC online system | VP Finance |

#### AML Compliance Program

| Component | Detail | Frequency |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| AML Compliance Officer | VP Finance designated as AMLCO | Ongoing |
| Employee AML training | All store managers, cashiers, AR staff, treasury staff | Annual |
| AML risk assessment | Company-wide assessment of money laundering risks | Annual |
| Independent AML audit | External review of AML controls | Biennial |
| Record retention | All CDD and transaction records retained for 5 years from transaction date | Ongoing |
| AMLC registration | Company registered with AMLC as covered person | One-time + renewal |

### POL-FIN-010: Intercompany Transaction Processing Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All intercompany transactions between Tahanan Depot entities  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** Intercompany, Payables, Receivables, General Ledger

#### Policy Statement

All intercompany transactions between Tahanan Depot entities must be processed at arm's-length pricing, properly authorized, documented, and reconciled monthly. This policy ensures compliance with BIR transfer pricing regulations (RR No. 2-2013), accurate consolidation, and transparent financial reporting.

#### Intercompany Pricing (Supplement to POL-FIN-006)

| Transaction Type | Pricing Method | Documentation Required | Review Frequency |
|-----------------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Goods (SCM → Retail) | Cost + 8% markup | Cost build-up analysis, comparable uncontrolled price study | Annual |
| IT Services (Digital → Others) | Cost + 12% markup | Time-and-materials records, cost allocation basis | Annual |
| Property Lease (Property → Others) | Market rate (independently appraised) | Independent appraisal report | Annual |
| Management Fees (Holdings → Others) | 2% of revenue | Management services agreement, allocation methodology | Annual |
| Shared Services (HR, Finance) | Actual cost / headcount ratio | Cost allocation schedule | Monthly |
| Interest on Intercompany Loans | BSP reference rate + 1% spread | Loan agreement, interest computation | Monthly |

#### Intercompany Transaction Processing Rules

1. All intercompany transactions must reference a valid Intercompany Service Agreement (ISA) or Intercompany Supply Agreement (ICSA).
2. Intercompany invoices generated in Oracle Fusion Intercompany module; no manual journal entries for intercompany transactions.
3. Intercompany invoice approval follows standard approval matrix (POL-FIN-001).
4. Monthly intercompany reconciliation: All entities must reconcile intercompany payables/receivables by the 10th of the following month.
5. Intercompany differences > PHP 50,000 must be investigated and resolved within 5 business days.
6. Annual transfer pricing documentation prepared per BIR RR No. 2-2013 and filed with BIR by the statutory deadline.
7. Intercompany settlements: Net settlement on a monthly basis via PESONet; cash settlement within 30 days of reconciliation.
8. Intercompany elimination entries generated automatically by Oracle FCC during consolidation close.

#### Intercompany Settlement Schedule

| Activity | Timing | Responsible |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Intercompany invoices issued | 5th business day of month | Originating entity |
| Intercompany reconciliation | 10th business day of month | All entities |
| Dispute resolution | 15th business day of month | VP Finance |
| Net settlement calculation | 16th business day of month | Treasury |
| Cash settlement | 20th business day of month | Treasury |
| Intercompany elimination (FCC) | Day 4 of monthly close | Consolidation team |

### POL-SCM-006: Third-Party and Vendor Risk Management Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All third-party vendors, suppliers, contractors, and service providers  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** Supplier Management, Advanced Controls, Security

#### Policy Statement

All third parties engaged by Tahanan Depot are subject to risk-based assessment and ongoing monitoring proportional to the nature, scope, and criticality of the services provided. This policy ensures third-party risks (operational, financial, cybersecurity, compliance, reputational) are identified, assessed, mitigated, and monitored throughout the vendor lifecycle.

#### Vendor Risk Classification

| Risk Tier | Criteria | Assessment Frequency | Approval Authority |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 — Critical | System access to Oracle Fusion or POS; handles PII; single-source dependency; annual spend > PHP 20M | Annual comprehensive assessment | VP level + CIO + CLO |
| Tier 2 — High | Access to non-sensitive systems; handles confidential business data; annual spend PHP 5M–20M | Annual assessment | VP level + CIO |
| Tier 3 — Medium | Limited system access; handles non-sensitive data; annual spend PHP 1M–5M | Biennial assessment | Director level |
| Tier 4 — Low | No system access; handles only public information; annual spend < PHP 1M | Initial assessment only | Manager level |

#### Risk Assessment Domains

| Domain | Assessment Criteria | Weight |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Financial Stability | Credit rating, audited financial statements, years in operation | 20% |
| Operational Capability | Track record, references, capacity, quality certifications | 20% |
| Information Security | Security certifications (ISO 27001), security questionnaire, penetration testing, incident history | 25% |
| Compliance and Legal | Regulatory compliance, litigation history, sanctions screening, D&B/World-Check screening | 20% |
| Business Continuity | BCP/DRP existence, recovery capabilities, insurance coverage | 15% |

#### Vendor Onboarding Process

1. Vendor completes risk assessment questionnaire (tier-based).
2. Due diligence checks: financial, legal, sanctions, PEP screening.
3. Security assessment for Tier 1-2 vendors: security questionnaire, evidence review, possible on-site assessment.
4. Contract review: includes data protection clauses, security requirements, SLAs, termination rights, audit rights.
5. Risk rating assigned and documented in Oracle Supplier Management.
6. Approval per tier authority matrix.
7. Vendor onboarding and access provisioning (if applicable).

#### Ongoing Monitoring

| Activity | Frequency | Owner |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Performance scorecard review | Quarterly | Business owner + Procurement |
| Financial health check | Annual | Finance |
| Security assessment | Per tier schedule | IT Security |
| Sanctions/PEP screening | Quarterly (Tier 1-2), Annual (Tier 3-4) | Legal / Compliance |
| Contract renewal review | 90 days before expiration | Business owner + Procurement |
| Incident tracking | Ongoing | Business owner |
| Annual vendor risk reassessment | Per tier schedule | Procurement + IT Security + Legal |

#### Vendor Offboarding

1. Business owner initiates offboarding with 30-day notice.
2. All company data returned or destroyed (data destruction certificate required).
3. System access revoked within 24 hours of service termination.
4. Final performance assessment documented.
5. Vendor deactivated in Oracle Supplier Management.
6. Post-termination obligations monitored (confidentiality, non-compete).

### POL-HR-012: Contractor and Contingent Workforce Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All contractual workers, agency temps, project-based workers, and consultants  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** HCM (Contingent Worker tracking), Time and Labor, Security

#### Policy Statement

Tahanan Depot engages approximately 1,575 contractual/agency workers for warehouse operations, store promotions, project-based construction, and specialized services. This policy ensures proper classification, compliance with Philippine labor laws (DOLE Department Order 174), cost management, and security of contingent workforce.

#### Worker Classification

| Category | Definition | Typical Roles | DOLE Compliance |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Agency-Deployed | Workers employed by a labor agency, deployed to Tahanan Depot | Warehouse laborers, store promo staff, janitorial | DO 174-compliant agency agreement required |
| Project-Based | Workers hired for a specific project with defined duration | Construction workers, IT project consultants | Per-project contract;不超过 6 months without regularization risk |
| Seasonal | Workers hired for peak periods | Holiday season store staff, typhoon relief logistics | Seasonal contract (6 months max) |
| Independent Contractor | Self-employed professionals providing specialized services | IT consultants, legal counsel, audit services | Properly documented as contractor (no employer-employee relationship indicators) |
| Intern / Apprentice | Students or trainees gaining experience | TESDA apprentices (per CSR program), OJTs | Per TESDA rules or DOLE internship guidelines |

#### Engagement Rules

1. All agency deployments require a DO 174-compliant service agreement between Tahanan Depot and the licensed labor agency.
2. Agency workers must not exceed 6 months in the same position without rotation or conversion review.
3. Project-based workers engaged only for specific, time-bound projects; contract duration and scope clearly defined.
4. Independent contractors must demonstrate independence: control over work methods, own tools/equipment, ability to work for multiple clients.
5. No contractor/agency worker may supervise regular employees.
6. Contractor/agency workers clearly identified with different ID badges and uniforms.
7. Contractor/agency workers do not receive Tahanan Depot employee benefits; benefits provided by agency or per contract terms.

#### Access and Security

| Access Type | Regular Employee | Agency/Contractor | Approval |
|------------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Physical access (store/warehouse) | Standard | Badge with limited zones | Store/Warehouse Manager |
| Oracle Fusion access | Full (per role) | Limited (time entry only) | IT + Hiring Manager |
| POS system access | Yes | No (except agency cashiers with supervisor override) | Store Manager |
| Corporate network (VPN) | Yes | Limited (specific project need) | IT + VP |
| Building access (HQ) | Card + biometric | Visitor/temporary badge | Facilities + Hiring Manager |

#### Performance Management

1. Agency performance monitored and communicated to agency monthly.
2. Underperforming agency workers replaced within 5 business days upon request.
3. Contractor performance reviewed per contract milestones.
4. Annual agency performance review covering: deployment quality, replacement speed, compliance, worker satisfaction.

### POL-HR-013: Social Media and Digital Communications Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and agents  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** Marketing (Social Media), HR

#### Policy Statement

Tahanan Depot recognizes the role of social media in business and personal communication. This policy provides guidelines for employees' use of social media in both professional and personal capacity to protect the company's reputation, brand, and confidential information.

#### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Social Media | All online platforms for user-generated content: Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter), TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, blogs, forums, review sites, messaging apps |
| Company Social Media | Official Tahanan Depot accounts managed by Marketing/PR team |
| Personal Social Media | Employee's personal accounts used in individual capacity |

#### Personal Social Media Guidelines

1. Employees must disclose their employment with Tahanan Depot when posting about company-related matters.
2. Employees must not share confidential company information, trade secrets, financial data, customer data, or internal communications on social media.
3. Employees must not post content that is defamatory, harassing, discriminatory, or threatening toward colleagues, customers, suppliers, or competitors.
4. Employees must not represent themselves as official spokespersons of Tahanan Depot unless authorized by the CMO.
5. Employee complaints about workplace issues should be directed through HR channels, not posted on social media.
6. Employees must not post photographs or videos from store backrooms, warehouses, or restricted areas without authorization.
7. Employees must not use the Tahanan Depot logo, branding, or trademarks without authorization.
8. Personal social media activity during work hours is permitted only during breaks.

#### Company Social Media (Official Accounts)

1. All official social media accounts managed by VP Digital Marketing team.
2. Content calendar approved weekly by VP Digital Marketing.
3. All public posts reviewed by at least one team member before publishing.
4. Crisis communications on social media require CMO and/or CLO approval before posting.
5. Customer complaints received via social media routed to Oracle CX Service for tracking and response.
6. Social media advertising managed per marketing budget and approval process (POL-FIN-001).
7. Influencer partnerships require Marketing VP approval and written agreement.

#### Employee Advocacy Program

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Program name | Tahanan Advocates |
| Eligibility | Regular employees with 6+ months tenure |
| Content provided | Pre-approved content on company news, promotions, CSR activities, career opportunities |
| Platform | LinkedIn (primary), Facebook (optional) |
| Incentive | Recognition points in Oracle ME; quarterly "Top Advocate" award |
| Guidelines | Only share pre-approved content; no personal opinions on company matters |

#### Violations

1. First violation: Verbal counseling and social media training.
2. Second violation: Written warning.
3. Third violation: Suspension (3-5 days) or termination depending on severity.
4. Severe violations (disclosure of material non-public information, harassment, defamation): Immediate termination.
5. Social media violations that cause financial or reputational damage to the company may result in legal action.

### POL-IT-005: Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) Policy

**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Applies To:** All locations (120 stores, 4 warehouses, corporate HQ)  
**Oracle Fusion Module:** HCM Workforce Health & Safety, Assets, Inventory

#### Policy Statement

Tahanan Depot is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all employees, contractors, customers, and visitors, and to minimizing the environmental impact of its operations. This policy establishes the EHS framework beyond the workplace safety provisions in POL-HR-007 and warehouse safety in POL-SCM-004.

#### Environmental Management

1. Compliance with all Philippine environmental laws: RA 6969 (Toxic Substances), RA 8749 (Clean Air), RA 9003 (Solid Waste), RA 9275 (Clean Water).
2. Environmental permits maintained current for all locations (LGU, DENR, EPA).
3. Waste segregation mandatory at all locations: Biodegradable, Recyclable, Residual, Hazardous.
4. Hazardous waste (paint, chemicals, batteries, e-waste) disposed only through DENR-licensed transporters and treaters.
5. Water and energy consumption monitored monthly per location; reduction targets set annually.
6. Environmental incidents (spills, releases, contamination) reported within 1 hour to VP Facilities and CSR team.

#### Occupational Health

1. Annual medical examination for all employees (company-provided, per POL-HR-007).
2. Pre-employment medical examination required for all new hires.
3. Ergonomic assessments for office workers annually; adjustments provided as needed.
4. Occupational health surveillance for workers exposed to hazardous substances (paint, chemicals, wood dust).
5. Hearing conservation program for warehouse workers exposed to noise above 85 dBA.
6. Respiratory protection program for workers handling dusty materials (cement, wood, insulation).
7. Heat stress prevention program for warehouse and store receiving staff.
8. Mental health and wellness program: EAP counseling, stress management workshops, work-life balance initiatives.

#### Safety Management

1. Safety committees established at each location per DOLE DO 198 requirements.
2. Monthly safety inspections at all locations; findings tracked and remediated.
3. Incident investigation for all recordable incidents within 48 hours; root cause analysis required.
4. Safety training requirements:
   - General safety orientation: All employees (upon hiring, annual refresher)
   - Fire safety and evacuation: All employees (quarterly drill)
   - Hazardous materials handling: Relevant staff (annual)
   - Forklift/powered equipment: Certified operators (annual recertification)
   - First aid: Minimum 2 trained first-aiders per location (annual recertification)
   - Emergency response: Safety committee members (semi-annual drill)
5. Near-miss reporting encouraged; near-miss data analyzed for prevention.
6. Safety KPIs tracked monthly: Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR), Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR), Near-Miss Reporting Rate.

#### Safety KPI Targets

| Metric | Target | Industry Benchmark | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| TRIR (per 200,000 hours) | < 2.0 | 3.0-4.0 | HCM Workforce Health & Safety |
| LTIFR (per 200,000 hours) | < 1.0 | 1.5-2.5 | HCM Workforce Health & Safety |
| Near-miss reporting rate | > 5 reports/month per location | Varies | Safety reporting system |
| Safety training completion | 100% | 95% | Oracle Learning |
| Safety inspection completion | 100% | 90% | Facilities tracking |

Third parties with access to Tahanan Depot IT systems, networks, data, or physical facilities must meet minimum security requirements to protect company information assets. This policy supplements POL-SCM-006 (Third-Party and Vendor Risk Management Policy) with IT-specific security requirements.

#### Access Provisioning for Third Parties

1. Third-party system access requested via IT Service Desk with business justification.
2. Access provisioned with minimum-privilege principle; time-limited where applicable.
3. VPN access for remote third parties; no direct internet-facing access to internal systems.
4. All third-party access logged and monitored.
5. Third-party accounts reviewed quarterly by IT Security.
6. Access revoked within 24 hours of contract termination or project completion.
7. Shared/third-party accounts prohibited; each individual must have unique credentials.

#### Cloud Service Providers (SaaS/PaaS/IaaS)

1. All cloud service providers undergo security assessment before engagement.
2. Data residency requirements confirmed: Customer data stored within Philippines or approved jurisdictions.
3. Sub-processor disclosure and approval required.
4. Right-to-audit clause included in all cloud service contracts.
5. Annual security review of cloud service providers (SOC 2 report, ISO 27001, penetration test results).
6. Oracle Cloud (OCI) is the primary cloud platform; new cloud providers require CIO approval.

#### Incident Response for Third-Party Breaches

1. Third party notifies Tahanan Depot IT Security within 24 hours of discovering a security incident affecting Tahanan data.
2. Tahanan Depot IT Security assesses impact and activates incident response plan if needed.
3. Third party cooperates with investigation and provides all requested evidence.
4. Cost of incident response borne by third party (per contract terms).
5. Repeated or severe incidents result in vendor termination.
