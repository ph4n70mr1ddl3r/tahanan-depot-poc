# 14 — STORE OPERATIONS GUIDE

## 14.1 Store Operations Overview

This document provides the comprehensive operational framework for Tahanan Depot retail stores beyond the transactional SOPs covered in the Standard Operating Procedures. It covers store standards, merchandising, customer service, workforce management, and performance management.

## 14.3 Visual Merchandising Standards

### 14.3.1 Store Layout Principles

| Zone | Purpose | Key Requirements |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Entrance / Power Aisle | Create strong first impression; seasonal/promotional displays | Change monthly; high-impact signage; trend products |
| Department Zones | Organized by product category; logical flow | Clear signage; consistent fixture types; adequate aisle width (min 1.5m) |
| End Caps | Promotional and seasonal items; high-visibility | Change bi-weekly; tied to current promotions |
| Impulse Areas (near checkout) | Small tools, accessories, fasteners, batteries, seasonal items | Pre-packed; self-service; high-margin items |
| Pro Desk (Contractor Service) | Dedicated B2B service area | Separate entrance preferred; dedicated POS terminal; product catalog |
| Garden Center | Live plants, outdoor furniture, seasonal garden items | Outdoor/semi-outdoor area; irrigation; shade structure |
| Lumber Yard | Building materials, plywood, steel, cement | Covered outdoor area; forklift access; heavy-duty racking |
| Receiving / Backroom | Inventory storage, receiving, returns processing | Organized by department; clear labeling; safety compliance |

### 14.3.2 Merchandising Standards

| Element | Standard | Frequency of Review |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Planogram compliance | 95% adherence to planogram (set by Merchandising team in Oracle) | Weekly walk-through by Department Supervisor |
| Shelf labeling | 100% items with correct price labels; synced with Oracle Pricing | Daily verification; auto-verified on POS |
| Product facing | All items front-faced; no gaps on shelves | Hourly (sales associate responsibility) |
| Signage | Clean, undamaged, current promotional signage only | Daily check; change with promotion schedule |
| Display maintenance | Displays fully stocked; damaged items removed; fixtures clean | Daily |
| Promotional displays | Set per Merchandising directive; photographed for compliance | Per promotional calendar |
| Cross-merchandising | Related items displayed together (e.g., paint + brushes + rollers) | Per Merchandising directive |
| Demo / display units | Functional displays for power tools, appliances; safety-compliant | Monthly maintenance check |
| Stock levels (sales floor) | Minimum presentation quantities maintained; replenished from backroom | Continuous (stock associate responsibility) |
| Cleanliness | All areas clean, organized, free of debris, properly lit | Daily; hourly in high-traffic areas |

## 14.4 Customer Service Standards

### 14.4.1 Customer Service Principles

1. **Greet every customer** within 10 seconds of entering the department.
2. **Approach and assist** — Proactively offer help; guide to products; provide expert advice.
3. **Product knowledge** — Every associate must complete product knowledge training for their department (Oracle Learning).
4. **First contact resolution** — Resolve customer issues at the first point of contact whenever possible.
5. **Pro Desk priority** — Contractor/B2B customers receive priority service at the Pro Desk.

### 14.4.2 Service Level Targets

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Customer greeting time (upon entering department) | < 10 seconds | Mystery shopper (quarterly) |
| Checkout wait time | < 5 minutes (standard); < 3 minutes (express lane) | POS queue monitoring |
| Customer complaint first response | < 4 hours (Level 1-2); < 1 hour (Level 3-4) | Oracle CX Service |
| Customer complaint resolution | < 24 hours (Level 1-2); < 4 hours (Level 3-4) | Oracle CX Service |
| NPS (Net Promoter Score) | > 50 | Monthly survey |
| CSAT (Customer Satisfaction) | > 90% | Monthly survey |
| Loyalty enrollment rate | > 30% of eligible transactions | POS Analytics |
| Product availability (in-stock rate) | > 95% for active SKUs | Inventory Analytics |
| B2B order fulfillment | > 98% on-time delivery | Order Management Analytics |

### 14.4.3 Customer Engagement Programs

| Program | Description | Frequency | Oracle Fusion Touchpoint |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------------|
| DIY Workshops | In-store workshops (basic plumbing, painting, electrical safety, tiling) | Monthly per store | CX Marketing (event management) |
| Pro Club Events | Contractor networking, product demonstrations, exclusive offers | Quarterly per region | CX Sales (event management), Marketing |
| Product Demonstrations | Live product demos (power tools, appliances) | Weekly (weekends) | — |
| Community Events | Store anniversary celebrations, community service, school supply drives | Annual + as scheduled | CX Marketing, Narrative Reporting |

### 14.4.4 Customer Complaint Escalation Matrix

| Scenario | Level | First Response | Resolution | Escalation |
|----------|-------|---------------|------------|------------|

## 14.5 Store Workforce Management

### 14.5.1 Scheduling Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Customer traffic-aligned | Staff schedules aligned with store traffic patterns (from POS analytics and Oracle Workforce Scheduling) |
| Minimum staffing | Minimum staffing levels per format defined below; never fall below |
| Flex coverage | 10-15% of hours allocated to flexible coverage (peak hours, call-outs, promotions) |
| Cross-training | All sales associates trained on POS checkout for backup cashiering |
| Break compliance | Mandatory breaks per DOLE: 1-hour meal break for 8-hour shift; morning and afternoon breaks per company policy |
| Holiday scheduling | Double pay per Labor Code; volunteer basis first; rotation if insufficient volunteers |

### 14.5.2 Minimum Staffing by Shift

| Role | Opening Shift (8 AM - 2 PM) | Mid Shift (10 AM - 6 PM) | Closing Shift (2 PM - Close) |
|------|----------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| Manager on Duty | 1 (Asst. Manager or Manager) | 1 (Store Manager preferred) | 1 (Asst. Manager or Manager) |
| Department Supervisors | 2 (covering priority departments) | 3-4 | 2 |
| Sales Associates | 5-8 | 8-10 | 5-8 |
| Cashiers | 2-3 | 3-4 | 2-3 |
| Stock Associates | 2 | 2 | 2 |

### 14.5.3 Workforce Scheduling Process (Oracle Workforce Scheduling)

| Step | Activity | Frequency | Owner |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | AI-generated schedule recommendation based on traffic forecast, sales forecast, and labor budget | Weekly | Oracle Workforce Scheduling |
| 2 | Store Manager reviews and adjusts AI schedule | Weekly | Store Manager |
| 3 | Schedule published to Oracle ME (employee self-service) | 1 week in advance | Store Manager |
| 4 | Shift swap / cover requests via Oracle ME | Ongoing | Employees (with manager approval) |
| 5 | Actual attendance tracked via POS/biometric → Time & Labor | Daily | Time & Labor |
| 6 | Schedule vs. actual variance report | Weekly | Store Manager |
| 7 | Labor productivity analysis (sales per labor hour) | Weekly | Store Manager + Regional Director |

## 14.7 Store Replenishment and Inventory Operations

### 14.7.1 Store Inventory Management Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible | Oracle Fusion Touchpoint |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------------------------|
| Receiving and put-away | Per delivery | Stock Associate | POS Receiving Module → Oracle Inventory (via OIC) |
| Sales floor replenishment | Continuous (min. 3x daily) | Stock Associate | POS stock level monitoring |
| Spot check counting (20 SKUs/day) | Daily | Stock Associate | Oracle Inventory (cycle count) |
| Department cycle count | Weekly (rotating departments) | Stock Associate + Supervisor | Oracle Inventory (cycle count) |
| Full store cycle count | Monthly (rotating sections) | Inventory team | Oracle Inventory (cycle count) |
| Damaged goods processing | Daily | Stock Associate | POS → Oracle Inventory (adjustment) |
| Return-to-vendor processing | Weekly | Stock Associate + AP | POS → Oracle Inventory + Payables |
| Backroom organization | Daily | Stock Associate | — |
| Safety stock level monitoring | Daily (system alerts) | Store Manager | SCP + Inventory alerts |
| Dead stock review | Monthly | Store Manager + Category Manager | Inventory Analytics (dead stock report) |

### 14.7.2 Replenishment Methods

| Method | Trigger | Used For | Automation Level |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| Auto-replenishment (SCP-driven) | Supply planning MRP run | Fast-moving, regular replenishment items | Fully automated (warehouse picks and ships) |
| Store-requested transfer | Store Manager identifies need | Stock-outs, unexpected demand, special orders | Manual request → Oracle Order Management → transfer order |
| Cross-dock delivery | Supplier delivery pre-allocated to stores | Fast-moving items; seasonal items; promotional stock | Automated (WMS cross-dock allocation) |
| Direct vendor delivery | PO placed directly to store | Large/bulky items (cement, steel, lumber); local vendors | PO in Oracle Purchasing; received in POS |
