# 15 — CHANGE MANAGEMENT & TRAINING

## 15.1 Organizational Change Management (OCM) Framework

### 15.1.1 OCM Vision

The Oracle Fusion Cloud implementation and custom POS deployment represent the most significant operational transformation in Tahanan Depot's history. The OCM framework ensures that people, processes, and technology align to achieve sustained adoption and business value realization.

### 15.1.2 OCM Governance

```
OCM STEERING COMMITTEE
├── Chair: CHRO
├── Members: CEO, CFO, COO, CIO, CMO, VP Enterprise Apps, VP HR Business Partners
├── Frequency: Monthly during implementation; quarterly post go-live
└── Responsibilities:
    ├── Approve change management strategy and plans
    ├── Monitor adoption metrics and escalate resistance
    ├── Resource allocation for change management activities
    └── Executive sponsorship of key change initiatives
│
CHANGE MANAGEMENT LEAD
├── Reports to: CHRO (with dotted line to CIO)
├── Team: 2 Change Management Specialists + 1 Training Manager + 1 Communications Specialist
└── Responsibilities:
    ├── Develop and execute OCM plan
    ├── Stakeholder analysis and engagement
    ├── Communication planning and execution
    ├── Training strategy and curriculum design
    ├── Resistance management
    ├── Adoption measurement and reporting
    └── Sustained change reinforcement
│
CHANGE CHAMPIONS NETWORK
├── 2 per store = 240 store champions
├── 2 per warehouse = 8 warehouse champions
├── 5 corporate champions
└── Role: Advocate change, provide peer support, escalate feedback, assist training
```

### 15.1.3 Change Impact Assessment

| Change Area | Impact Level | Affected Population | Key Changes |

### 15.2.1 Stakeholder Analysis

| Stakeholder Group | Size | Readiness | Influence | Strategy |
|-------------------|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Executive Team (C-suite) | 6 | Mixed | Very High | Executive briefings; dashboard demos; value realization tracking |
| VP / Director level | 20 | Generally positive | High | Co-design workshops; process ownership assignment; early access |
| Store Managers | 120 | Mixed (concern about disruption) | High | Hands-on pilots; peer learning; address concerns directly; celebrate wins |
| Finance team | 100 | Generally positive | Medium | Detailed training; process walkthroughs; parallel run support |
| Supply Chain team | 200 | Mixed (concern about complexity) | Medium | Hands-on WMS training; phased rollout; lab simulations |
| Store associates | 1,800 | Low readiness (change fatigue risk) | Medium (critical mass) | Simple, visual training; peer-led; POS intuitive UX; gamification |
| Cashiers | 720 | Low readiness (POS anxiety) | Medium | Extensive hands-on POS training; sandbox practice; minimal disruption to checkout |
| Warehouse staff | 350 | Low readiness (technology adoption) | Medium | RF device training; visual guides; buddy system; patience with learning curve |

| Corporate staff | 200 | Mixed | Low-Medium | Self-paced e-learning; Oracle ME adoption; help desk support |

### 15.2.2 Resistance Management Plan

| Resistance Type | Indicators | Mitigation Strategy |
|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Lack of awareness | "I didn't know about this change" | Multi-channel communication campaign; mandatory town halls |
| Fear of job loss | "Will the system replace me?" | Clear messaging: technology augments, not replaces; reskilling commitment |
| Comfort with current state | "The old way works fine" | Demonstrate pain points of current state; show benefits of new system |
| Change fatigue | "Another change?" | Minimize simultaneous changes; celebrate quick wins; manage pace |
| Lack of confidence | "I won't be able to learn this" | Hands-on training; sandbox practice; peer support; patient rollout |
| Passive resistance | Non-compliance; workarounds; reverting to old processes | Monitor adoption metrics; manager accountability; coaching; escalate if persistent |
| Active resistance | Vocal opposition; undermining adoption | One-on-one engagement; address specific concerns; involve in solution; escalate if necessary |

## 15.4 Training Strategy

### 15.4.1 Training Approach

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Role-based | Training tailored to each role's specific system usage (not module-based) |
| Blended learning | Combination of instructor-led, e-learning, hands-on practice, and on-the-job coaching |
| Phased | Training delivered just-in-time before each module go-live (not all at once) |
| Multi-modal | Classroom, virtual, e-learning, video, quick reference guides, POS simulator |
| Practice-heavy | Maximum hands-on time; minimum lecture; sandbox/playpen environments |
| Localized | Filipino-language materials for store and warehouse operations; English for corporate |
| Measurable | Pre and post assessments; completion tracking in Oracle Learning |

## 15.6 Post-Implementation Support Model

### 15.6.1 Support Tiers

| Tier | Scope | Team | Channels | Hours |
|------|-------|------|----------|-------|
| **Tier 0 — Self-Service** | FAQs, quick reference guides, video tutorials, Oracle ME knowledge base | Content maintained by Training Manager | Oracle ME Knowledge, intranet | 24/7 |
| **Tier 1 — Store/Warehouse** | Basic troubleshooting, process questions, password reset | Change Champions + Store/Warehouse supervisors | In-person, Oracle ME chat, phone | Store/warehouse operating hours |
| **Tier 2 — Help Desk** | Application issues, data corrections, access requests, integration issues | IT Help Desk (Tahanan Digital L1/L2) | Phone, email, Oracle CX Service | 6 AM - 10 PM daily |
| **Tier 3 — Specialist** | Complex configuration, business rule changes, bug fixes | Oracle Fusion Functional Leads + POS Dev Team | Internal ticketing (Oracle CX Service) | Business hours |
| **Tier 4 — Oracle Support** | Product defects, platform issues, feature requests | Oracle Support (via Oracle Support portal) | Oracle Support portal, SI partner | Per Oracle SLA |

### 15.6.2 Hypercare Support (Per Go-Live Phase)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Duration | 4 weeks per go-live phase |
| Team | Dedicated floor support: 2 per pilot store (POS), 2 per warehouse (SCM), 4 corporate (ERP/HCM) |
| Schedule | All operating hours during first 2 weeks; peak hours only during weeks 3-4 |
| Escalation | Immediate access to Tier 2 and Tier 3 support |
| Daily standup | 15-minute daily standup with hypercare team, store managers, and IT |
| Issue tracking | All issues logged in Oracle CX Service; daily report to OCM Steering Committee |
| Knowledge capture | New issues and solutions added to Tier 0 knowledge base daily |

### 15.6.3 Steady-State Support Transition

| Milestone | Criteria | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Hypercare exit | Issue volume < 5 per day per location; no Severity 1 issues in past 7 days | 4 weeks post go-live |
| Reduced support | Floor support withdrawn; L1/L2 handles issues via help desk | 6 weeks post go-live |
| Steady state | All support via normal help desk channels; adoption metrics at target | 12 weeks post go-live |
| Continuous improvement | Regular optimization reviews; advanced training; feature adoption | Ongoing |
